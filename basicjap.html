Basic Japanese

Before You Begin...

Japanese consist of three different basics. Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji.

Hiragana is the most basic alphabet of Japanese. Katakana is used to describe the word or sentences coming from outside of Japan. Kanji is used to shorten words when you write. When you speak Japanese, you use the pronunciation of Hiragana. When you write, almost all sentences contain Hiragana and Kanji. Let’s see each Japanese basics one by one.
