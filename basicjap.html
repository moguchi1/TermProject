Before You Begin...

Japanese consist of three different basics: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji.

Hiragana is the most basic alphabet of Japanese.
Katakana is used to describe the word or sentences coming from outside of Japan.
Kanji is used to shorten words when you write.

When you speak Japanese, you use the pronunciation of Hiragana. When you write, almost all sentences contain Hiragana and Kanji.
Mastering Kanji requires a lot of works, so let's first study Hiragana and Katakana!

Did you realize something??

You may realize that most of letter contain vowel of a i u e o (あ い う え お) and combine it with a consonant (k s t n h m y r w) can produce the basic alphabet and its sound.

For example, vowel "a" (あ) + consoant "k" creates "ka" (か). (check the word with the Generator below)
